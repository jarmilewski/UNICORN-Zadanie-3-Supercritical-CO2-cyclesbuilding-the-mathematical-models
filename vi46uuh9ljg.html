The models were simulated in various power, temperature, and pressure ranges (see Table \ref{tab:DiscussionComparison}). The power ranges of the turbomachinery are not very well fitted to the heat exchanger. Heat flow transferred in the heat exchanger is two orders of magnitude larger than turbomachinery. Modeling of heat exchangers does not require specific characteristics, thus the model can be easy used for lower heat ranges, whereas for turbomachinery we used specific characteristics of real devices.  The created models give the results with error below 10%, what seems to be reasonable. 
The paper presents the models of basic elements of supercritical carbon dioxide cycles. We built the following models: heat exchanger, CO2 compressor and CO2 expander. The models were validated based on the real characteristics taken from literature references. The characteristics were implemented into used software (Ebsilon). There are small deviations between the calculated values and provided by experiments.
Due to relatively small sizes of turbomachinery used in the supercritical CO2 cycles, the is no possible to use the Flugel--Stodola \cite{Milewski_2012} equation for modeling turbine flow parameters and it is necessary to use the real characteristics.
