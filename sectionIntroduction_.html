@Intruducing renewable power sources into the electric grid to deal with the variability of the wind and sun resources. The surplus wind energy can be delivered to the grid to optimize the trade-off between purity and cleaning degree. 
The introduction of renewable power sources into the grid requires storage capacity to deal with variability of the wind and sun resources. There are developed various systems for energy storage such as e.g. liquid/compressed air energy storage \cite{Krawczyk_2018,ISI:000415769300002}, thermal energy storage  \cite{Le_ko_2017}   . One of the most prospective energy storage concepts seems to be power-to-gas-to-power  \cite{Kotowicz_2017}. The power-to-gas-to-power  system is consist of gas turbine  \cite{ISI:000431450000009,Zhuang_2017} which is supplied from a distributed electrolysis installations  \cite{Garc_a_Cl_a_2018,Nadar_2018,Zarei_Senseni_2017,Fukuzumi_2017,Chen_2017,Zhang_2017,Olivier_2017} , powered by the wind farms located a short distance from the potential construction site of the gas turbine.  The high temperature fuel cells are being considered as one of the next generation electric power sources because of its eco-friendliness and high efficiency advantages, hence fuel cells such as SOFC, \cite{Kupecki_2018,Prokop_2018,Genc_2018,Azizi_2018,De_Lorenzo_2018,Ferrel_lvarez_2018,ISI:000417079400026,Peksen_2018,ISI:000433378300009,Dillig_2018,2017,Kupecki_2017,Zheng_2017,Barelli_2017}, H+SOFC \cite{Dzierzgowski_2018,Danilov_2017,ISI:000413004200059} and MCFC \cite{Davoodi_2018,ISI:000430563900009,Jienkulsawad_2018,Wu_2017,Accardo_2017,Cheon_2013,Samanta_2017,Czelej_2017}.
